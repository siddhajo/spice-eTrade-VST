Privacy Policy

Vandanmedu Spices Trading LLP (VSTL)
Effective Date: 1 July 2026 | Last Updated: 1 July 2026

Vandanmedu Spices Trading LLP ("VSTL", "we", "us", "our") operates a cardamom auction business based in Pampupara, Idukki, Kerala, India. We use a mobile application ("VSTL eTrade") to record auction lot data and communicate with registered sellers. This Privacy Policy explains what personal information we collect, why we collect it, how we use it, and the rights you have over your data.

This policy is issued in compliance with the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (India) and the requirements of the WhatsApp Business Platform (Meta Platforms Ireland Ltd).

1. Who We Are

VSTL is a Limited Liability Partnership engaged in the auction and trading of cardamom and other spices. We act as the Data Fiduciary responsible for the personal data described in this policy.

2. Information We Collect

We collect only the information necessary to operate our auction business and settle payments with sellers:

  • Seller identification: Name, address, place, PIN code, state.
  • Contact details: Mobile number, WhatsApp number, email address (optional).
  • Statutory identifiers: GSTIN, PAN (where applicable).
  • Payment details: Bank account number, IFSC code, account holder name.
  • Auction lot data: Grade, number of bags, litre weight, gunny weight, net weight, sample weight, gross weight, moisture percentage, reserved price, receipt number.
  • Transaction data: Auction date, sale price, payment status.

We do not collect data from children. Our platform is intended solely for adult sellers registered with our auction business.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information collected only for the following purposes:

  • To register and identify sellers participating in our auctions.
  • To record and process auction lots.
  • To calculate and settle payments to sellers.
  • To generate receipts, invoices, and account statements.
  • To send transactional WhatsApp notifications (auction results, payment confirmations, receipt copies).
  • To meet legal and statutory obligations under Indian tax and commercial laws (GST, TDS, Income Tax).

We do not use your data for advertising, marketing, or promotional communication unless you have separately provided explicit consent.

4. WhatsApp Business Platform

We use the WhatsApp Business Platform provided by Meta Platforms Ireland Ltd to send transactional and utility messages to sellers. These messages include auction receipts, payment confirmations, and account statements.

When you provide your WhatsApp number to us, you consent to receiving such transactional messages. We do not send marketing broadcasts through WhatsApp. Meta's own privacy practices are governed by their WhatsApp Privacy Policy.

5. Sharing of Information

We do not sell, rent, or trade personal data. We share information only with the following parties, and only to the extent necessary:

  • Meta Platforms Ireland Ltd: For delivery of WhatsApp transactional messages.
  • Our bank and payment processors: For settling payments to sellers.
  • Government authorities: Where disclosure is required by law (GST, Income Tax, court orders).
  • Cloud infrastructure providers: For securely hosting our application and databases.

6. Data Retention

We retain personal and transactional data for a minimum of 7 (seven) years as required under the Indian Income Tax Act, 1961 and the Goods and Services Tax Act, 2017. After this period, data is either securely deleted or anonymised for statistical purposes.

7. Data Security

We use industry-standard safeguards to protect your data. These include encryption of data in transit (HTTPS/TLS), restricted access controls, secure authentication, and regular backups. No system is perfectly secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security, but we take reasonable steps to protect your information.

8. Your Rights

Under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you.
  • Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Request deletion of your data (subject to legal retention obligations).
  • Withdraw consent for WhatsApp communications at any time.
  • Nominate another person to exercise your rights in the event of death or incapacity.
  • Register a grievance with our Grievance Officer.

To exercise these rights, contact us using the details in Section 11 below.

9. Withdrawing WhatsApp Consent

You may stop receiving WhatsApp messages at any time by replying "STOP" to any of our messages, or by contacting us directly. Once withdrawn, we will cease sending WhatsApp messages within a reasonable time, though we may continue to contact you through other means for essential account and payment matters.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy from time to time to reflect changes in law or our practices. Updated versions will be posted at this URL with a revised "Last Updated" date. Continued use of our services after such updates constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
